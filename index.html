The Core Problem

How do you automatically tell if a trending topic is a real news event — or just internet noise?

Real Event
  • Sustained media coverage over multiple days
  • Article velocity accelerates organically
  • Multiple independent sources report it
  • Verifiable in the GDELT database
Internet Noise
  • Peaks and dies within hours
  • Confined to a single social platform
  • No mainstream news source coverage
  • Absent from GDELT database

05

Advanced ML: LDA Theory

Documents belong to multiple topics simultaneously.
LDA finds hidden thematic structure rather than just counting words.

Step 1: Topic Mix

Sample structural distribution θ from Dirichlet(α).

Ex: 60% Politics, 30% Health

Step 2: Topic Per Word

For each word block, sample topic z from Multinomial(θ).

Ex: word 1 → Politics

Step 3: Pick the Word

Sample final word directly from topic's vocabulary β.

Ex: Politics → "president"

The Static Limitation: LDA treats 2012 and 2022 articles identically. It possesses no native temporal tracking. This explicitly motivates our upcoming feature engineering.

06

Novel Feature Engineering

Temporal Weight

Dynamic recency weighting breaks LDA's static assumption.

w(d) = log(1+Δt) / max(log(1+Δt))
Category Velocity

Scales structural growth rate accurately at publication time.

V(t) = [N(w_t) - N(w_{t-1})] / N(w_{t-1})
Text Richness

Isolates breaking news via semantic density mappings.

R(d) = |Unique| / (|Total| + 1)

Rigorous Failure Analysis

The Anomaly: The COVID & Court topic statistically peaks in 2014 — five full years before COVID-19 physically existed.

2014: ACA Legal Battles

Top words: state, health, court, people

Context: Affordable Care Act mandates fighting through federal appellate courts.

2020: COVID-19 Pandemic

Top words: state, health, court, people

Context: Global pandemic driving state lockdowns and subsequent court challenges.

Why LDA Failed: Bag-of-words strips context. Identical vocabulary equals a Cosine Similarity of ~1.0.

Phase 2 Matrix: SBERT will computationally map full sentence semantics—encoding these completely separate contexts into isolated embedding space.

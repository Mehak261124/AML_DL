\begin{abstract}
We address distinguishing fleeting social media trends from significant real-world events by tracking semantic velocity across longitudinal text corpora. Using 11,000 HuffPost articles (2012-2022), we apply TF-IDF baseline extraction and LDA topic modeling, engineering three novel temporal features. LDA discovers COVID-19 and Trump/President topics completely unsupervised. Failure analysis mathematically traces topic conflation to LDA's bag-of-words assumption, motivating Phase 2 SBERT embeddings.
\end{abstract}

\vspace{-0.3cm}
\section{INTRODUCTION}
The exponential expansion of digital media has ushered in an era of unprecedented information overload, rendering it functionally impossible to manually distinguish superficial, fleeting social media trends from substantive, lasting real-world events. To navigate this noise systematically, we introduce and formalize \textit{semantic velocity} as our core mathematical concept. While standard topic models provide robust thematic extraction, they fundamentally fail when applied to continuous temporal streams because they treat all documents as temporally identical, ignoring the momentum of breaking news. As foundational work by Allan et al. \cite{allan1998} established, event detection requires chronological contextualization. By modeling text alongside its publication velocity, our methodology provides dynamic isolation of acute global occurrences.

\section{LITERATURE REVIEW}
Our architectural approach bridges classic probabilistic frameworks with modern contextual theories. Building an academic trajectory:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Allan et al. (1998):} The foundational Topic Detection and Tracking (TDT) pilot study formally established event detection parameters \cite{allan1998}. However, it relied heavily on simple word overlap and standard cosine similarity, notably lacking explicit continuous temporal modeling.
    \item \textbf{Blei, Ng, and Jordan (2003):} Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) revolutionized probabilistic topic modeling, evaluating documents structurally as discrete finite mixtures of topics \cite{blei2003}. Despite mapping complex structures, LDA is fundamentally \textit{static}---it ignores time completely, assuming full exchangeability.
    \item \textbf{Reimers \& Gurevych (2019):} Addressing static token limitations, Sentence-BERT (SBERT) introduced highly contextual sentence embeddings \cite{reimers2019}. Unlike bag-of-words paradigms, SBERT mathematically encodes positional meaning.
    \item \textbf{Grootendorst (2022):} Leveraging these semantic encodings, BERTopic combined SBERT architectures with density-based clustering to formulate a robust modern topic modeling pipeline \cite{grootendorst2022}.
    \item \textbf{McInnes et al. (2018):} Enabling high-dimensional clustering within BERTopic, Uniform Manifold Approximation and Projection (UMAP) preserves topological data structures during mandatory dimensionality reduction \cite{mcinnes2018}.
\end{enumerate}
Ultimately, our work bridges the historic gap between classical generative models (LDA) and modern contextual methods (BERTopic) by explicitly engineering temporal features to address LDA's structural temporal absence.

\section{NOVEL FEATURE ENGINEERING}

\begin{figure*}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{model_plots/feature_engineering.png}
\caption{\small Three Novel Features: Temporal Weight (left), Category Velocity (center), Text Richness Ratio (right)}

\textbf{Feature 1 — Temporal TF-IDF Weighting:}
Standard LDA mathematically treats a document published in 2012 identically to one released in 2022. We counteract this using a logarithmic recency weight:
\begin{equation}
w(d) = \frac{\log(1 + \Delta t_d)}{\max_d \log(1 + \Delta t_d)}
\end{equation}
where $\Delta t_d$ calculates the active days elapsed since corpus start. Testing the subset, the calculated mean weight reaches $0.88$. Crucially, this operation directly violates and subsequently corrects LDA's fundamental exchangeability assumption by prioritizing temporally contiguous momentum mapping.

\textbf{Feature 2 — Category Velocity Score:}
\begin{equation}
V(c,t) = \frac{N(c,W_t) - N(c,W_{t-1})}{N(c,W_{t-1}) + \epsilon}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Feature 3 — Text Richness Ratio:}
\begin{equation}
R_d = \frac{|\text{Unique Words in } d|}{|W_d| + 1}
\end{equation}
Generating a corpus subset mean richness density of $0.86$, this feature capitalizes on human behavioral dynamics.

\section{LDA METHODOLOGY}
Our probabilistic matrix deploys Latent Dirichlet Allocation utilizing structured parameter optimization: $K=10$ distinct autonomous topics, bounded by a finite threshold limits of $\text{max\_iter}=20$, deploying batch learning procedures controlled uniformly by $\text{random\_state}=42$, mapped onto a baseline $\text{CountVectorizer}$ enforcing $\text{max\_features}=5000$.

\begin{equation}
p(\theta,z,w|\alpha,\beta) = \prod_{d=1}^{D} p(\theta_d|\alpha) \prod_{i=1}^{N_d} p(z_{d,i}|\theta_d) p(w_{d,i}|z_{d,i},\beta)
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}
\begin{split}
\mathcal{L}(\gamma,\phi;\alpha,\beta) &= E_q[\log p(\theta|\alpha)] + E_q[\log p(z|\theta)] \\

\end{split}
\end{equation}

The extracted Covid \& Court specific topic peak functionally calculates maximum velocity mapping arrays scaling predominantly in 2014---notably evaluating exactly five full operational years distinctly prior entirely before the COVID-19 biological virus variant formally originated.

Explicit numerical mathematical calculation traces the boundary origin identically to precise operational overlaps. Specifically contextualizing parameters during 2014, massive continuous Affordable Care Act legal boundary challenge publications comprehensively dominated widespread news circulations mapping extensive density parameters for identical key words: \textit{state, health, court, people}. Subsequently bridging toward 2020, massive expansive COVID health pandemic sequences dominated similar publications structurally deploying exact overlapping terminologies.

Assessing overall outcomes, our LDA methodology effectively isolated major occurrences precisely, notably capturing the independent ascension of both COVID-19 and dominant US political topics exclusively through unsupervised algorithms.
